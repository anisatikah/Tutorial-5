1 Statistical Terminology

Given the following statement:
'A study was conducted at a local college to analyze the average cumulative GPAs of students who graduated last year.'

Determine the suitable terminology used in statistics to describe every phrase below.
Hint: (Population / Statistic / Parameter / Sample / Variable / Data)
(a)
all students who graduated from the college last year
Answer (a): Population
The entire group of interest in the study.
(b)
the average cumulative GPA of students in the sample who graduated from the college last year
Answer (b): Statistic
A numerical measurement describing a characteristic of a sample.
(c)
the average cumulative GPA of all students who graduated from the college last year
Answer (c): Parameter
A numerical measurement describing a characteristic of the entire population.
(d)
a group of students who graduated from the college last year, randomly selected
Answer (d): Sample
A subset of the population selected for study.
(e)
the cumulative GPA of one student who graduated from the college last year
Answer (e): Variable
A characteristic or attribute that can assume different values.
(f)
Possible students' CGPA such as 3.65, 2.80, 1.50, 3.90 etc.
Answer (f): Data
The actual values (measurements or observations) collected for the variable.

(a) First Quartile Q₁

(d) Standard Deviation

(e) Pearson's Coefficient of Skewness
Mean = Σfx / Σf = 338/24 = 14.0833

9 Mean, Standard Deviation & Coefficient of Variation

In a mid-semester examination, the total marks that Aishah obtained for all her 10 subjects, ΣM = 650, while the total squared of her mark, ΣM² = 43000.
(a) Find the mean and standard deviation for her marks.
(b) Calculate the coefficient of variation for her marks.
(a) Mean & Standard Deviation
ΣM = 650, ΣM² = 43000, n = 10
Mean = ΣM / n = 650 / 10 = 65
Variance s² = ΣM²/(n−1) − (ΣM)²/[n(n−1)]
= 43000/9 − (650)²/(10 × 9)
= 4777.78 − 4694.44
Variance = 83.3334
Standard Deviation s = √83.3334
Mean = 65  |  Standard Deviation s = 9.1287
(b) Coefficient of Variation
CV = (s / Mean) × 100%
= (9.1287 / 65) × 100%
Coefficient of Variation = 14.0442%
A CV of ~14% indicates moderate variability in Aishah's marks across subjects.

(b) Find the median and mode for the allowances. By comparing the value of mean, mode and median, state the type of this distribution. [7 marks]
Median
Median is the (N/2)th = 16th observation → in class 200 – 300
ClassfCum. f
100 – 2001212

Median = L₂ + (c₂/f₂) × (N/2 − Σf_b)
= 200 + (100/7) × (16 − 12)

Mode
Modal class: 100 – 200 (highest frequency = 12)
Mode = L + (d₁/(d₁ + d₂)) × c
L = 100, d₁ = 12 − 0 = 12 (no class before), d₂ = 12 − 7 = 5, c = 100

Type of Distribution

Comparing: Mode < Median < Mean

Mode = 170.58 < Median = 257.14 < Mean = 275
∴ The distribution is positively skewed (skewed to the right)
When Mode < Median < Mean, the tail extends to the right. The peak is on the left side with the tail tapering off to the right.
Skewness ReferenceCLO 2

Understanding Distribution Shapes

Positively Skewed (Q10 Result)

Mode < Median < Mean
Peak on LEFT, tail extends RIGHT
Sk > 0

Negatively Skewed

Mean < Median < Mode
Peak on RIGHT, tail extends LEFT
Sk < 0

Symmetric (Normal)

Mean = Median = Mode  |  Sk = 0  |  Bell-shaped, balanced on both sides
Formula SummaryReference

Key Formulae

Mean (Grouped)

x̄ = Σfx / Σf

Mode (Grouped)

Mode = L + (d₁/(d₁+d₂)) × c
d₁ = f_modal − f_before
d₂ = f_modal − f_after

Median (Grouped)

Q₂ = L₂ + (c₂/f₂)(N/2 − Σf_b)

Quartiles

Q₁ → N/4
Q₂ → N/2
Q₃ → 3N/4

Percentile

Pₖ = L + (c/f)(kN/100 − Σf_b)

Variance

s² = Σfx²/(n−1) − (Σfx)²/[n(n−1)]

Std Dev & CV

s = √Variance
CV = (s / x̄) × 100%

Pearson's Skewness

Sk = (Mean − Mode) / s
Sk > 0 → Positive
Sk < 0 → Negative
